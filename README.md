# Project Context
In 2012, MIT and Harvard University co-founded edX, a non-profit open online learning platform. This project analyzes four years of data (2012–2016) encompassing:

  - 290 Harvard and MIT online courses.  

  - 250,000 certifications.  

  - 4.5 million participants.  

  - 28 million participant hours.


Research Question

  - "Do certification rates differ significantly across different course subject areas?"


Hypotheses:

We are specifically investigating whether certification rates in Computer Science courses vary significantly between MIT and Harvard. 

  - Null Hypothesis ($H_0$): There is no significant difference in certification rates between MIT and Harvard courses in Computer Science.
      
  - Alternative Hypothesis ($H_1$): There is a significant difference in certification rates between MIT and Harvard courses in Computer Science.


Population of Interest:

  - The study focuses on learners enrolled in online courses offered by MIT and Harvard University on the edX platform between the years 2012 and 2016.
